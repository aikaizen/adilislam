About

I teach people how to think about AI product strategy.

I'm Adil Islam. I've shipped AI products at three companies — from a 50-person startup to one of the largest technology companies in the world. What I've learned: most organizations don't fail at AI because the technology isn't ready. They fail because they think about it wrong.

What I believe

Most teams start with "we have GPT-5" and work backward. The ones that ship start with a painful, specific workflow and work forward. The best AI products feel boring. They do one thing reliably, 10,000 times in a row. The first agent should make humans faster, not replace them. Trust is earned in increments.

I write about AI agents, agent context, context engineering, and agentic systems — what it takes to ship AI products that actually work in production, not just in demos.
